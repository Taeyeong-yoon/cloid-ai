Cowork Mastery

Cowork is not just multiple agents in one screen. It is a way to design role split, responsibility boundaries, and handoff logic. This textbook organizes cowork, skill, and plugin flow from a practical work-composition perspective.

Common cowork mistakes

Believing more agents automatically means better performance
Giving every agent the same job without role boundaries
Skipping handoff and review stages and jumping straight to execution
Core sentence

Good cowork is not just many agents working. It is clarity about who does what, when the baton gets passed, and who reviews the result.

Design principle

If the split reason, handoff rules, and review ownership are unclear, cowork usually becomes slower and less reliable.

Section A

Cowork base model

Cowork becomes easiest to understand when modeled through three axes: roles, handoff, and review.

R

H
Handoff

Q
Review

Role split

This is where work is split across different specialties such as research, drafting, editing, or checking. If roles overlap too much, confusion grows faster than speed.

Handoff

Cowork stays stable when the next role always receives the right structure and criteria. The baton can be passed manually or through structured output, but the rule must stay fixed.

Review

If nobody owns the final review and its criteria, collaboration may move faster while quality drifts. The final layer of cowork is always review design.

Section B

Single agent vs cowork

The key is knowing when cowork is needed and when a single agent is enough.

When one agent is better

  • When the task is short and ends as one document
  • When handoff overhead costs more than the work itself
  • When review is simple

When cowork is better

  • When research, drafting, and review are clearly different kinds of work
  • When intermediate outputs need structured handoff
  • When quality criteria have multiple layers
Section C

Three-role panel

For textbook-style collaboration, these three roles appear most often.

Research

Researcher

Builder

Builder

Designs the textbook structure and flow

Review

Reviewer

Checks quality and accuracy

Researcher role

The research role should focus on collecting raw material. If it also takes over explanation and structure, the value of cowork becomes blurred.

Builder role

The builder turns collected material into learning order and interaction structure. It designs flow more than raw information.

Reviewer role

The reviewer checks accuracy, difficulty level, and communication quality at the end. Without this role, cowork output tends to drift.

Section E

First lab: cowork for textbook production

A three-role split like the one below lets you feel cowork immediately in textbook production.

  1. The researcher collects source material, cases, and core points.
  2. The builder turns them into section structure, quiz flow, and memo flow.
  3. The reviewer checks difficulty level and accuracy.
  4. Finally, combine the work into one textbook HTML.
Section F

Governance and control

Cowork is governed more by control than by raw feature count.

Must be explicit

  • Input and output of each role
  • Handoff timing
  • Final review ownership

What to avoid

  • Overlapping responsibility across all roles
  • Direct auto-execution without review
  • Multi-agent design without a clear split reason
